ADH Mechanism of Action on Renal Tubules:
ADH binds to specific V2 receptors on the late distal tubules, collecting tubules, and collecting ducts
.
This binding activates adenylyl cyclase, which increases the formation of cAMP in the tubular cell cytoplasm
.
cAMP activates protein kinases that cause the phosphorylation of elements in special vesicles containing aquaporin-2 (AQP-2)
.
These vesicles insert into the apical (luminal) cell membranes, creating highly water-permeable pores
.
This allows water to be rapidly reabsorbed from the tubular fluid into the renal interstitial fluid by osmosis
.Anti-Inflammatory Actions:
Stabilizes lysosomal membranes, preventing the release of proteolytic enzymes
.
Decreases capillary permeability, preventing loss of plasma into tissues
.
Decreases migration of white blood cells into the area and reduces phagocytosis
.
Suppresses the immune system, particularly T lymphocytes
.
Attenuates fever by reducing the release of interleukin-1

The condition arising from insulin deficiency is Diabetes Mellitus
.
CHO Metabolism: Causes hyperglycemia (elevated blood glucose) because cells cannot take up glucose
. When blood glucose exceeds the renal threshold (~200 mg/dL), glucose is lost in the urine (glycosuria)
.
Fat Metabolism: Results in extreme lipolysis and the release of free fatty acids
. This leads to increased plasma cholesterol and the formation of ketone bodies (acetoacetic acid, β-hydroxybutyric acid, acetone), causing ketosis and metabolic acidosis
.
Protein Metabolism: Causes stop in protein storage and rapid protein wasting, leading to muscle weakness, weight loss, and increased urea excretion
.

. It stimulates the growth of the breast mammary glands, promotes an extensive ductile system, and causes fat deposition in the stroma to give the breasts mass

Progesterone: Once the ductal system is developed, progesterone (acting synergistically with estrogen) promotes the development of lobules and alveoli
. It causes the alveolar cells to proliferate, enlarge, and take on secretory characteristics, though it does not initiate actual milk secretion
.
Prolactin: Secreted by the anterior pituitary, it is the principal hormone for milk production (lactogenesis)
. It also acts as a necessary background hormone for initial ductal developme
Oxytocin: Secreted by the posterior pituitary, its primary role is milk ejection (the "let-down" reflex)
. It causes the myoepithelial cells surrounding the breast alveoli to contract, expressing milk into the ducts
Mammogenesis: The process of breast development and the growth of the mammary glands into milk-secreting organs
.
Lactogenesis: The initiation of milk secretion by the prepared alveolar cells
.
Lactation: The overall process encompassing both the secretion of milk and its ejection from the breasts
